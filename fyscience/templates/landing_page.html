Why is this allowed?

Paying a journal to make your research publications open access is not the only way you can liberate your science from paywalls and expensive journal subscriptions. Many publishers actually allow publishing a freely accessible version of the paper, separate from the journal.

Depending on the journal's policy, you can upload your work to an institutional archive (e.g. your university library), a preprint server like arXiv or your personal website. The policies usually distinguish between the submitted, accepted and published version of a document. Most often you are allowed to re-publish the accepted or submitted version, without having to pay the publisher.

Most policies require you to link the freely accessible version back to the paywalled version. This helps to make sure that other publications end up citing the main version of the paper. At the same time, having a free copy available, more people are able to find your research.

Technical Background

Free Your Science builds upon a set of wonderful existing services that try to make scientific works more accessible. We heavily rely on their databases being correct and appreciate the service they offer to the world.

  • unpaywall.org lets us determine if Open Access version exist for a given DOI
  • Semantic Scholar lets us find an author's papers and additional Open Access versions
  • ORCID API powers our search by ORCID
  • Crossref lets us link papers to their ISSN, but also underpins some of the above services
  • Sherpa API maintains a database of publisher's Open Access policies

It is important to note that all information on freeyourscience.org is meant to facilitate, to educate fellow authors about which Open Access re-publication possibilities to look into. No information shown here necessarily forms any basis for legal claims towards authors, publishers or any other party.

If you are curious about how we end up at the number at the top of the page, please feel free to check out the analysis pipeline we used to calculate that number in December 2020 over in the Free Your Science GitHub repository.

While our main goal is to provide this web based tool for fellow humans, like authors and interested people, we are also working on a machine readable interface to the information we merge from the different sources. You can have a look at our OpenAPI specification.

Should you want to use our API or in case you still feel like you are missing features, please contact us via team@freeyourscience.org

Our Motivation

{# - background: -- enjoy collaborative spirit of globally networked academic communities -- foster accessibility and collaboration -- can't stand when information that should be freely accessible is unnecessarily hidden behind paywalls - strategy: -- tbd - for authors: -- enable to discover the potential they have with past publications - for institutions: -- call us #}

We are two open science enthusiasts. Having worked in different positions of academia, from being an author to supporting institutions with grant writing and being part of the publisher side of things, we have come to appreciate the open and collaborative spirit of the academic community but also came to acknowledge the constraints that still restrict the accessibility of scientific results.

Our goal is to foster accessibility and collaboration in the beautiful, globally networked academic communities. Also, we just can't stand when information that should be freely accessible is unnecessarily hidden behind paywalls.

Accordingly, our strategy is to:

  • create awareness about the potential of publisher's Open Access policies
  • help authors understand which research they can free from paywalls

Looking forward, we hope that research institutions, funders and other interested parties can use this tool to discover the potential that they have to drastically increase the amount of Open Access publications.
